// about me

The honest backstory

I got genuinely obsessed with LLMs around 2023 — not because they were trending, but because I realized they were actually useful. Like, properly useful. That feeling of getting a model to do something complex that would've taken days to code manually? That never got old.

My grad school years at ASU were basically one big playground. I'd pick a problem I found annoying, then build something to fix it. StyloGuard came from being frustrated that AI detectors were easily gamed. The Energy Simulator came from wondering why nobody made data center carbon costs feel real.

Now at CMCI, I'm doing the most technically intense work of my life — building healthcare AI that actually has to work reliably. No room for hallucinations. That constraint has made me a way sharper engineer than any side project ever did.

Outside of building: I'm usually reading about agent architectures, breaking things in Jupyter notebooks, or thinking about how to make AI systems less brittle. Still figuring it all out — just faster than before.
